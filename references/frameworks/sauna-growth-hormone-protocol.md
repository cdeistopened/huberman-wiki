## When to Use

Use this when someone asks about sauna for health, deliberate heat exposure, growth hormone and sauna, cardiovascular mortality data, heat shock proteins, or how to use a sauna for recovery and longevity. Huberman covers two distinct sauna protocols: a general health protocol supported by large Finnish epidemiological studies, and a specific growth hormone spike protocol from a smaller but frequently cited study.

## The Protocol

Deliberate heat exposure through sauna use produces cardiovascular adaptations that mimic moderate exercise — increased heart rate, plasma volume expansion, improved endothelial function — along with hormonal responses (growth hormone, cortisol reduction) and activation of heat shock proteins and the FOXO3 longevity pathway.

### Protocol 1: General Health and Longevity

This protocol is derived from the large Finnish epidemiological studies (Kuopio Ischemic Heart Disease Risk Factor Study) that tracked sauna use and mortality outcomes over decades.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Temperature | 80-100 degrees Celsius (176-212 degrees Fahrenheit) |
| Duration per session | 5-20 minutes |
| Frequency | 2-7 times per week |
| Minimum effective frequency | 2-3 times per week |

**Mortality data from the Finnish studies:**

| Frequency | Cardiovascular Mortality Reduction (vs. 1x/week) |
|-----------|---------------------------------------------------|
| 2-3 times per week | 27% reduction |
| 4-7 times per week | 50% reduction |

> "People who did sauna four to seven times a week were 50% less likely to die of a cardiovascular event compared to people who did sauna just once a week." — Andrew Huberman, EQ3GjpGq5Y8.md

**Physiological adaptations from regular sauna:**
- Heart rate rises to 100-150 bpm during sessions (mimics moderate cardiovascular exercise)
- Plasma volume expansion (more blood volume available for circulation)
- Increased stroke volume (heart pumps more blood per beat)
- Heat shock protein activation (cellular repair and stress resilience)
- FOXO3 pathway activation (associated with longevity in centenarian populations)
- Cortisol reduction with regular use

### Protocol 2: Growth Hormone Spike

This protocol comes from a specific study designed to maximize acute growth hormone release through heat stress. It is substantially more demanding than the general health protocol.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Temperature | 80 degrees Celsius (176 degrees Fahrenheit) |
| Duration | 30 minutes per session |
| Sessions per day | 4 sessions (with brief cooling periods between) |
| Total sauna time per day | 2 hours |

**Growth hormone response:**

| Day | Growth Hormone Increase |
|-----|------------------------|
| Day 1 | 16-fold increase |
| Day 3 | ~3-4x increase |
| Day 7 | ~2-3x increase |

> "Subjects that did this two hour a day, 80 degree Celsius protocol experienced 16-fold increases in growth hormone." — Andrew Huberman, EQ3GjpGq5Y8.md

The growth hormone spike diminishes rapidly with repeated exposure because the response is driven by novelty and stress — the body adapts. To preserve the large spike, use this protocol infrequently: once per week or less.

### How to Implement

**For general health (most people):**
1. Use a dry sauna or infrared sauna at 80-100C (176-212F).
2. Stay for 5-20 minutes per session.
3. If starting, begin with 5-10 minutes and build tolerance over weeks.
4. Aim for 3-4 sessions per week minimum (ideally 4-7 per week based on the mortality data).
5. Hydrate before, during, and after. Electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) are important.
6. Can be done on training days or rest days. If done after resistance training, note the cold exposure caveat — sauna (heat) does not have the same hypertrophy-blunting effect as cold.

**For growth hormone (periodic use):**
1. Set aside a half-day for 4 x 30-minute sauna sessions at 80C.
2. Between sessions, cool down with cool (not cold) air or a lukewarm shower. Do not use ice-cold water — the goal is heat accumulation, not contrast therapy.
3. Use this protocol no more than once per week to maintain the magnitude of the GH response.
4. This protocol is uncomfortable. It is not relaxation — it is deliberate stress exposure.

**Heat acclimatization (for athletes, from Dr. Duncan French / UFC):**
1. Start with 15 minutes (can split into 3 x 5-minute blocks).
2. Build to 30-45 minutes continuous over several weeks.
3. Approximately 14 exposures are needed for significant heat acclimatization.
4. Begin 8-10 weeks before a competition or event in hot conditions.

### Caveats

- The 16-fold growth hormone increase is impressive but context-dependent. This is not comparable to the sustained GH levels of puberty or exogenous GH administration. It is a transient spike.
- Growth hormone from sauna diminishes rapidly with repeated use. The body adapts to the thermal stress. Infrequent use preserves the magnitude of the response.
- Sauna mimics cardiovascular exercise in some hemodynamic parameters but does not replace actual cardiovascular training. It is a supplement, not a substitute.
- Hydration and electrolyte management are critical. Significant sweating depletes sodium, potassium, and magnesium.
- People with cardiovascular conditions, pregnant women, and those on blood pressure medications should consult a physician before beginning a sauna protocol.
- Alcohol and sauna is a dangerous combination — the Finnish data showing sauna-related deaths primarily involved alcohol consumption.
- Infrared saunas operate at lower temperatures (approximately 50-60C) but may require longer sessions to achieve comparable core temperature elevation.

## Example

A 48-year-old woman has access to a dry sauna at her gym. She begins going 3 times per week after her resistance training sessions, staying for 12-15 minutes at 85C. After 4 weeks, she increases to 4 times per week and extends sessions to 18-20 minutes. She notices improved heat tolerance, better sleep on sauna days, and her resting heart rate drops from 72 to 66 bpm over two months. Once per month, she does the growth hormone protocol on a Saturday: 4 x 30 minutes at 80C with 15-minute cooling breaks between sessions.

## Output

After 4-8 weeks of consistent sauna use (3-4x/week, 15-20 min at 80-100C), expect:
- Improved cardiovascular markers (lower resting heart rate, improved blood pressure)
- Better heat tolerance (subjective comfort at temperatures that previously felt intolerable)
- Reduced cortisol levels with regular use
- Improved sleep quality (if done 2-3 hours before bed, not immediately before)
- Periodic growth hormone spikes when using the infrequent high-dose protocol
- Activation of heat shock proteins and FOXO3 longevity pathway
