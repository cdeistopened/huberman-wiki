## When to Use

Apply this framework when someone asks about sleep supplements, has trouble falling asleep or staying asleep, wants to improve sleep quality without prescription medication, asks about the Huberman sleep stack specifically, or wants to understand how to layer sleep supplements progressively.

## The Protocol

Huberman has used the same core sleep supplement stack nightly for over eight years. The stack has three tiers: the core trio taken every night, a rotating addition for deeper support, and an occasional booster. Each component works through a different mechanism, and they can be taken individually or in combination.

The philosophy: start with one supplement at a time so you can identify what works and what causes side effects. Build up to the full stack only after confirming individual tolerance.

### Tier 1: The Core Trio (Every Night)

**Magnesium L-Threonate** -- 145 mg. Crosses the blood-brain barrier; chosen over other magnesium forms for this reason. The anchor of the stack.

**Apigenin** -- 50 mg. Derived from chamomile. Works on GABA and chloride channel pathways. Also has a mild cortisol-reducing effect.

**L-Theanine** -- 100-400 mg. Start at 100 mg. Amino acid from tea that promotes calm without sedation. However, some people should leave this out entirely.

> "145 milligrams of magnesium threonate. 50 milligrams of apigenin. And 100 to 400 milligrams of theanine taken, again, alone or in combination with the other supplements mentioned in the stack many people find allows them to get really drowsy and fall asleep, sleep really deeply, and they feel much more refreshed the next day and they don't have a grogginess to them." -- Huberman

### Tier 2: Rotating Addition (Every Other Night)

**Myo-Inositol** -- 900 mg, every other night. Particularly effective for people who fall asleep fine but wake at 2-3 AM and cannot fall back asleep. Also provides a "long tail" of anxiety suppression the following day.

> "Every other night, I'll take 900 milligrams of myo-inositol in addition to mag threonate... what I find is not only does it greatly enhance my ability to fall asleep quickly, but if I wake up in the middle of the night... I find it very, very easy to fall back asleep." -- Huberman

### Tier 3: Occasional Booster (Every 3rd-4th Night)

**Glycine + GABA** -- 2 grams glycine + 100 mg GABA, every 3rd or 4th night only. Significantly enhances sleep onset but loses effectiveness with frequent use.

> "Every third or fourth night, I will take two grams of glycine and a hundred milligrams of GABA in addition to the standard sleep stack... the reason I only add glycine and GABA every third or fourth night is that if I take it too often, I find that the entire sleep stack doesn't work quite as effectively." -- Huberman

## Dosage & Timing

| Supplement | Dosage | Frequency | Timing |
|------------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Magnesium L-Threonate | 145 mg | Every night | 30-60 min before bed |
| Apigenin | 50 mg | Every night | 30-60 min before bed |
| L-Theanine | 100-400 mg | Every night (if tolerated) | 30-60 min before bed |
| Myo-Inositol | 900 mg | Every other night | 30-60 min before bed |
| Glycine | 2,000 mg | Every 3rd-4th night | 30-60 min before bed |
| GABA | 100 mg | Every 3rd-4th night | 30-60 min before bed |

**Stomach:** Ideally on a relatively empty stomach (no food for ~3 hours). Still works after late meals, but best absorbed without food competition.

**Rotation rule:** Inositol nights and glycine+GABA nights do not overlap. Huberman alternates them.

## Caveats

- **L-Theanine and vivid dreams.** Some people experience vivid, anxiety-producing dreams from theanine -- even at 100 mg. If this happens, drop theanine entirely and use only magnesium threonate + apigenin. The two-supplement version is still highly effective.
- **Magnesium GI sensitivity.** About 5% of people get diarrhea from magnesium threonate. Huberman is direct: "The proper dosage for you, in other words, would be zero milligrams." Switch to magnesium bisglycinate if this occurs.
- **Glycine + GABA must be limited.** Taking this combination nightly reduces the effectiveness of the entire stack. The every-3rd-or-4th-night limit is based on Huberman's direct experience.
- **Apigenin and estrogen.** Women of childbearing age should be aware that apigenin may have mild estrogenic effects.
- **Start single, build up.** Do not begin the full stack simultaneously. Start with magnesium threonate alone for several nights, then add apigenin, then consider theanine.
- **This replaces melatonin.** Huberman specifically recommends this stack instead of supplemental melatonin for nightly use (see the melatonin-warning framework for details).
- **Not a substitute for sleep hygiene.** Behavioral tools (light exposure, temperature, timing) remain more important than any supplement. Huberman always emphasizes behavioral interventions first.
