## When to Use

Apply this framework when someone asks about natural testosterone support, Tongkat Ali or Fadogia Agrestis supplementation, supplements for libido or sexual health, hormone optimization without TRT, or wants to understand the difference between these two compounds and how they work together.

## The Recommendation

Tongkat Ali and Fadogia Agrestis are two supplements Huberman has discussed for testosterone support. They work through complementary but distinct mechanisms, and both require blood work monitoring -- this is non-negotiable when modulating sex hormones.

### Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia Jack)

Tongkat Ali works by liberating bound testosterone. Most testosterone in the bloodstream is bound to sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG) and is unavailable for use. Tongkat Ali frees some of this bound testosterone, increasing the biologically active "free testosterone" fraction.

Huberman personally used Tongkat Ali from his mid-30s through mid-40s with blood-work-confirmed results. He also notes subtle anti-estrogenic and aphrodisiac effects.

> "There is some decent scientific literature to support the fact that it liberates some of the bound testosterone and allows more free testosterone to be available." -- Huberman

> "I got great results all through my mid-30s until mid-40s, and still with Tongkat Ali freeing some testosterone up -- my blood charts told me that worked for me, though it may not work for everybody." -- Huberman

### Fadogia Agrestis

Fadogia works at a different level entirely. Rather than freeing bound testosterone, it increases luteinizing hormone (LH) production from the pituitary gland. LH signals the testes (or ovaries) to produce more testosterone (or estrogen). This is upstream of where Tongkat Ali acts, making the two complementary.

> "Fadogia agrestis... at least according to the literature that I was able to find, can increase levels of luteinizing hormone and thereby levels of testosterone or levels of estrogen. The side effect profile of Fadogia agrestis hasn't really been documented, so it's a little unclear." -- Huberman

The pairing logic: Tongkat Ali frees what you have; Fadogia helps you produce more. Together, they address both supply and availability.

### Blood Work Requirements

Any supplement that modulates sex hormones requires periodic blood work. This is not optional. The panels should include:

| Marker | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Total testosterone | Baseline and response tracking |
| Free testosterone | The fraction Tongkat Ali specifically affects |
| SHBG | Sex hormone-binding globulin (inversely related to free T) |
| Estradiol (E2) | Testosterone can convert to estrogen via aromatase |
| LH / FSH | Fadogia's mechanism acts here; monitors pituitary response |
| PSA | Prostate-specific antigen; safety monitoring |
| Complete blood count | General health monitoring; testosterone affects red blood cells |

## Caveats

- **Blood work is mandatory.** Huberman is emphatic about this. Any tissue that undergoes rapid cell reproduction (prostate, testes, ovaries) is vulnerable to cancers that can be fueled by androgens. Monitoring hormone panels is essential for safety.
- **Effects are "far more subtle" than exogenous testosterone.** These supplements do not produce the dramatic effects of testosterone replacement therapy. They work within the body's natural regulatory capacity.
- **Tongkat Ali can cause insomnia.** Huberman notes that "some of the reported side effects are things like excessive alertness and insomnia if it's taken too late in the day." Take it in the morning or early afternoon.
- **Fadogia's side effect profile is poorly documented.** Huberman is transparent about this limitation. The research base for Fadogia is thinner than for Tongkat Ali, which warrants additional caution.
- **Negative feedback risk with Fadogia.** If testosterone rises too high through increased LH, the hypothalamic-pituitary axis can shut down LH production through negative feedback. This is why blood work and cycling matter.
- **Not for everyone.** Huberman's personal results were positive, but he explicitly notes: "it may not work for everybody." Individual hormonal baselines vary significantly.
- **Cycling is implied but not rigidly specified.** Many practitioners recommend cycling protocols (e.g., 8 weeks on, 2-4 weeks off) for hormone-modulating supplements, guided by blood work results.

## Output

When recommending Tongkat Ali and/or Fadogia, always specify:
1. The distinct mechanisms (Tongkat Ali frees bound T; Fadogia increases LH production)
2. That blood work is mandatory -- before starting, at 4-6 weeks, and periodically after
3. Tongkat Ali dosage range (400-800 mg/day) and morning timing
4. That Fadogia's safety profile is less well documented
5. The cancer safety consideration for hormone-sensitive tissues
6. That effects are subtle compared to exogenous testosterone
