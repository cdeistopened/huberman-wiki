## When to Use

Apply this framework when someone asks about Alpha-GPC supplementation, wants a supplement for focused work sessions, asks about acetylcholine and focus, wants to enhance power output for resistance training, or has concerns about TMAO from choline-based supplements.

## The Recommendation

Alpha-GPC (alpha-glycerophosphocholine) is an acetylcholine precursor that enhances both cognitive focus and physical power output. Huberman uses it occasionally before resistance training -- not daily, not as a foundational supplement, but as a targeted tool for specific high-demand sessions.

The mechanism is straightforward: Alpha-GPC increases acetylcholine availability. Acetylcholine is the neurotransmitter that narrows attentional focus and enhances the connection between motor neurons and muscles. More acetylcholine means sharper focus and greater force production.

The evidence for power output is concrete. One study found a 14% increase in power output from a single 300-600 mg dose. For cognitive function, Alpha-GPC has shown benefits particularly in older populations experiencing age-related cognitive decline.

But Huberman is careful to frame Alpha-GPC as a supplement-layer tool, not a first-line intervention.

> "I always emphasize behavioral tools first, then focusing on nutritional tools and on occasion using supplement-based tools to encourage increased levels of focus." -- Huberman

> "Alpha-GPC, which at least in the United States is sold over the counter, typically is taken in dosages of about 300 to 600 milligrams. That single dose has been shown to... enhance power output... A study noted a 14% increase in power output." -- Huberman

> "300 milligrams of Alpha GPC can be very effective. There are various sources for that... By no means am I saying that you need to take Alpha GPC." -- Huberman

## Dosage & Timing

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Dosage** | 300-600 mg (single dose) |
| **Form** | Capsule |
| **Timing** | Before workouts or focus sessions (30-60 minutes prior) |
| **Frequency** | Occasional use only; Huberman takes it "every once in a while" before resistance training |
| **With food** | Not critical |
| **Daily use** | Not recommended; this is an occasional tool |

### The TMAO Concern and Garlic Counterbalance

Alpha-GPC, like other choline-containing compounds, can increase levels of TMAO (trimethylamine N-oxide), a metabolite associated with cardiovascular risk in some research. Huberman's solution is simple: take 600 mg of garlic alongside Alpha-GPC. Garlic has been shown to offset the TMAO increase from choline-based supplements.

| Supplement | Dosage | Purpose |
|------------|--------|---------|
| Alpha-GPC | 300-600 mg | Acetylcholine precursor for focus and power |
| Garlic | 600 mg | TMAO offset |

## Caveats

- Alpha-GPC is an occasional tool, not a daily supplement. Huberman is explicit that behavioral tools (sleep, attention training, environmental design) come first, followed by nutritional approaches, with supplements as the last layer.
- The TMAO concern is real but manageable. Taking 600 mg of garlic alongside Alpha-GPC is Huberman's practical solution. Those with existing cardiovascular risk factors should discuss choline supplementation with their physician.
- Alpha-GPC increases acetylcholine, which enhances focus but can also increase anxiety or restlessness in some people. If you notice agitation rather than calm focus, lower the dose or discontinue.
- This is not a nootropic for daily cognitive enhancement. It is a targeted tool for specific sessions -- a hard workout, a demanding writing session, a complex problem-solving block.
- Some people find that 300 mg is sufficient. Start at the lower end before moving to 600 mg.
- Alpha-GPC can also improve cognitive function in older populations, but the research base for healthy young adults is thinner.

## Output

When recommending Alpha-GPC, always specify:
1. The 300-600 mg dosage range (start at 300 mg)
2. Occasional use only -- not daily
3. The garlic 600 mg counterbalance for TMAO
4. That behavioral and nutritional tools come before supplementation
5. Pre-workout or pre-focus-session timing
6. The 14% power output finding for resistance training context
