## When to Use

Use this when someone asks about hypnosis, has treatment-resistant stress or insomnia, wants to quit smoking, or is dealing with chronic pain. Clinical self-hypnosis is distinct from stage hypnosis — it enhances self-control rather than removing it. This protocol is based on the work of Dr. David Spiegel, Associate Chair of Psychiatry at Stanford, who appeared on Huberman Lab to discuss 40+ years of hypnosis research. Approximately two-thirds of adults are hypnotizable to some degree; about one-third are not. The protocol works through specific brain circuit changes, not suggestion or belief.

## The Protocol

### Step 0: Assess Hypnotizability (Spiegel Eye Roll Test)

Before beginning, determine whether self-hypnosis is likely to work for you:

1. Look up toward the ceiling as high as you can (eyes directed upward)
2. While keeping your eyes directed upward, slowly close your eyelids
3. The more white of the eye (sclera) visible as the eyelids close, the more hypnotizable you likely are
4. Scored on a 0-10 scale (Hypnotic Induction Profile)

Approximately 15% of adults are highly hypnotizable, 50% are moderately hypnotizable, and 33% are minimally or not hypnotizable. People with OCD tend to be less hypnotizable due to excessive self-evaluation interfering with the process.

### Step 1: Induction

1. Look up toward the ceiling
2. Close your eyes while keeping your gaze directed upward
3. Take one deep breath in
4. Exhale slowly
5. Let your eyes relax behind your closed lids
6. Imagine your body floating — a sensation of lightness or weightlessness

> "Self-hypnosis is a way of enhancing your control over your mind and your body." — Dr. David Spiegel, SOo4yNoaAoc.md

### Step 2: The Session

Once in the hypnotic state, apply it to the specific issue:

**For stress/anxiety:** Visualize a safe place. Imagine the stress as something physical that you can observe from a distance, then set down.

**For insomnia:** Imagine your body floating on water. Focus on the sensation of physical comfort and heaviness in the limbs. Let thoughts pass without engaging them.

**For pain:** Use the "split-screen" technique — on one side of an imaginary screen, observe the pain; on the other side, visualize your body comfortable and at ease. Shift attention to the comfort side.

**For trauma/PTSD:** The split-screen technique is particularly powerful here — imagine your body floating safely while viewing the traumatic event on the imaginary screen, as if watching a movie. This creates dissociative distance that allows processing without overwhelm.

**For smoking cessation:** Hypnosis quadruples smoking cessation effectiveness compared to cold turkey, according to the data Spiegel presented.

### Step 3: Duration

- Full sessions: 10-15 minutes
- "Refresher" sessions: as short as 1 minute
- Frequency: daily practice produces the strongest effects
- Two-thirds of users report feeling better even from 1-minute refresher sessions

> "I'm getting emails from people who said I haven't slept right in 15 years and now for the first time I'm listening to your app and I can sleep at night." — Dr. David Spiegel, SOo4yNoaAoc.md

### Step 4: Resources

- **Reveri app** (iOS App Store): Guided self-hypnosis sessions designed by Dr. Spiegel, ranging from 1-minute refreshers to 15-minute full sessions. Huberman endorsed this as the primary tool.
- **Professional referral:** Society for Clinical and Experimental Hypnosis; American Society for Clinical Hypnosis

### The Neuroscience

Brain imaging studies show three specific changes during hypnosis:

1. **Reduced dorsal anterior cingulate cortex activity** — decreased distraction and self-monitoring ("what am I doing? is this working?")
2. **Increased DLPFC-insula connectivity** — enhanced mind-body control (the prefrontal cortex gains more direct influence over bodily sensation)
3. **Inverse DLPFC-posterior cingulate connectivity** — reduced self-referential processing (less "self" getting in the way)

> "The issue is control. And hypnosis, which has this terrible reputation of taking away control, is actually a superb way of enhancing your control over mind and body." — Dr. David Spiegel, SOo4yNoaAoc.md

These circuit changes explain why hypnosis works for such diverse conditions — it is not targeting the symptom but enhancing the brain's regulatory capacity.

### Hypnosis vs. Meditation vs. NSDR

| Feature | Self-Hypnosis | Meditation | NSDR |
|---------|--------------|------------|------|
| Primary effect | Enhanced mind-body control | Improved focus and equanimity | Dopamine replenishment, rest |
| Attention type | Narrow focus + high suggestibility | Sustained concentration or open monitoring | Deliberate defocusing |
| Best for | Pain, insomnia, smoking, trauma, phobias | Daily focus, mood, attention | Sleep recovery, energy, stress |
| Duration | 1-15 min | 13+ min/day | 10-30 min |
| Active ingredient | Altered prefrontal-subcortical connectivity | Default mode network adjustment | Striatal dopamine increase |

### Caveats

- Clinical hypnosis is not stage hypnosis. It enhances control; it does not remove it.
- Approximately one-third of adults are not meaningfully hypnotizable — this is neurological, not a failure of effort or belief.
- Not appropriate for ignoring legitimate medical problems (e.g., using hypnosis to mask chest pain that could be cardiac).
- Deep trauma work should be done with a licensed professional, not self-directed.
- One randomized trial from Israel shows hypnosis added to PTSD treatment improves outcomes beyond treatment alone.
- Always see a licensed professional first for assessment of any serious condition.

## Example

A 42-year-old woman with chronic insomnia has tried sleep hygiene, melatonin, and CBT-I with limited results. She takes the Spiegel Eye Roll Test and scores 6/10 (moderately hypnotizable). She downloads the Reveri app and begins the insomnia-specific self-hypnosis protocol: 10-15 minutes before bed, imagining her body floating, focusing on physical comfort, letting thoughts pass without engagement. After 5 nights, she reports falling asleep within 20 minutes for the first time in years. She uses the 1-minute refresher if she wakes at 3am.

## Output

After 1-2 weeks of daily self-hypnosis practice (assuming adequate hypnotizability), a person should experience:
- Measurable improvement in the target condition (stress, pain, insomnia, or cravings)
- Enhanced sense of control over mind and body states
- Faster onset of relaxation response compared to baseline
- For smoking cessation: approximately 4x higher quit rate compared to willpower alone
- For insomnia: faster sleep onset and reduced middle-of-night waking
