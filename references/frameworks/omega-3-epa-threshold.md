## When to Use

Apply this framework when someone asks about fish oil supplementation, omega-3 fatty acids, EPA vs DHA, mood or depression support through nutrition, anti-inflammatory supplements, or cardiovascular health supplements. Also relevant when evaluating an existing supplement stack that includes fish oil.

## The Recommendation

Omega-3 supplementation is one of Huberman's foundational recommendations, but the critical detail most people miss is the EPA threshold. The mood and anti-inflammatory benefits of omega-3s depend on reaching at least 1,000 mg of EPA per day -- not total omega-3, not DHA, specifically EPA. Below that threshold, you are unlikely to see the documented effects on mood and inflammation.

The evidence base is substantial. In head-to-head comparison, 1,000 mg per day of EPA matched 20 mg of fluoxetine (Prozac) for reducing depressive symptoms. The combination of both produced a synergistic effect beyond either alone.

EPA works by producing resolvins and specialized pro-resolving molecules (SPMs), which actively resolve inflammation rather than merely suppressing it. This is a mechanistically distinct pathway from NSAIDs or other anti-inflammatory approaches.

Dr. Rhonda Patrick adds the longevity dimension: the omega-3 index (measured via red blood cell test) is a meaningful biomarker. People with an omega-3 index of 4% or lower had a five-year decreased life expectancy compared to those at 8%. Moving from 4% to 8% required approximately 2 grams of total omega-3 per day.

> "A comparison of 1,000 milligrams a day of EPA... compared to 20 milligrams of fluoxetine, which is Prozac. They found that they were equally effective in reducing depressive symptoms. What was really interesting is that the combination of 1,000 milligrams of EPA and fluoxetine had a synergistic effect in lowering depressive symptoms." -- Huberman

> "The threshold level seems to be about 1 gram, 1,000 milligrams of EPA... what's really important to look at is whether or not there's more than 1,000 milligrams of EPA because the EPA in particular is what's important here." -- Huberman

> "People who had an omega-3 index of 4% or lower had a five-year decreased life expectancy compared to people that had an 8% omega-3 index. For people in the 4% range to get to 8%... they needed to supplement with about 2 grams a day." -- Dr. Rhonda Patrick

## Dosage & Timing

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Minimum EPA** | 1,000 mg per day (the threshold for mood and anti-inflammatory effects) |
| **Total omega-3** | 2+ grams per day (Dr. Rhonda Patrick's recommendation for optimal omega-3 index) |
| **EPA:DHA ratio** | Natural ratio is approximately 2:1 EPA to DHA; do not seek pure EPA isolates |
| **Preferred form** | Triglyceride form (higher bioavailability); ethyl ester form acceptable but take with food |
| **Timing** | With food; time of day does not matter |
| **Storage** | Refrigerate to prevent oxidation |
| **Cycling** | None; daily use |
| **Testing** | Omega-3 index test (red blood cell measurement); takes 120 days to reflect changes |

### How to Read a Fish Oil Label

Most people look at total omega-3 content and stop there. Instead:
1. Find the EPA line specifically
2. Calculate how many capsules needed to reach 1,000 mg EPA
3. Check the IFOS (International Fish Oil Standards) certification: TOTOX score ideally under 6, at minimum under 10
4. Confirm triglyceride form if possible

## Caveats

- The 1,000 mg threshold is for EPA specifically, not total omega-3 and not DHA. Most fish oil capsules contain 300-500 mg EPA per capsule, meaning 2-3 capsules may be needed.
- Dr. John Kruse notes that a natural EPA:DHA ratio (roughly 2:1) is preferable to pure EPA isolates: "I think Mother Nature is probably smarter than any Harvard professor."
- Keep fish oil refrigerated. Oxidized fish oil may be counterproductive.
- The omega-3 index blood test reflects a 120-day average, so changes in supplementation take months to register.
- Fish oil is distinct from cod liver oil, which contains vitamin A and D in potentially problematic amounts at high doses.
- EPA can augment SSRIs -- this may be relevant for people already on antidepressants (consult prescribing physician).

## Output

When recommending omega-3 supplementation, always specify:
1. The 1,000 mg EPA minimum threshold and why EPA specifically matters
2. How to read the supplement label for EPA content
3. Triglyceride form preference and refrigeration requirement
4. The omega-3 index test as a way to track progress
5. That effects on the omega-3 index take ~120 days to appear
