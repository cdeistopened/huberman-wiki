## When to Use

Use this when someone asks for a complete weekly exercise template, wants to know how to balance cardio and strength training, asks "what does Huberman's training look like," or needs a structured program that covers all major fitness modalities. This is the master template from which individual protocols (Zone 2, HIIT, resistance training) are drawn. Huberman has followed a version of this split for 30+ years and published it as a free downloadable PDF.

## The Protocol

The foundational fitness protocol is a 7-day template that covers cardiovascular training (three modalities), resistance training (three sessions), and thermal stress. It is designed so that each muscle group gets hit directly once per week and indirectly once per week, while cardiovascular fitness is built across three distinct energy systems.

> "It describes the program that I've been following essentially for 30-plus years. And again, it's about three cardiovascular training sessions per week, three resistance training sessions per week." — Andrew Huberman, J2oqi41dCNc.md

### The 7-Day Template

| Day | Focus | Duration | Details |
|-----|-------|----------|---------|
| Day 1 | Long-duration cardio (Zone 2) | 60-90 min | Jog, hike, cycle, or row at conversational pace. Nasal breathing only. |
| Day 2 | Legs (resistance training) | 45-75 min | Squats, deadlifts, leg press, leg curls, calf raises. |
| Day 3 | Moderate cardiovascular training | 25-30 min | Moderate-to-high intensity. Jog, row, or bike at a pace above Zone 2. |
| Day 4 | Torso (resistance training) | 45-75 min | Chest, shoulders, back, neck. Presses, rows, pull-ups. |
| Day 5 | High-intensity interval training | 12-25 min | HIIT: sprints, assault bike, rowing intervals. See VO2 max protocol. |
| Day 6 | Smaller body parts (resistance training) | 45-75 min | Biceps, triceps, calves. Higher rep ranges (8-15 reps). |
| Day 7 | Thermal stress + active recovery | Variable | Deliberate cold exposure + deliberate heat exposure (sauna). Light walking. |

### The Cardiovascular Layer

Three distinct cardiovascular sessions serve three distinct adaptations:

1. **Day 1 — Zone 2 (long slow distance):** 60-90 minutes at an intensity where nasal breathing is sustainable. Builds capillary density, mitochondrial volume, and fat oxidation capacity. This session contributes the bulk of the 150-200 minutes per week Zone 2 target; the remainder comes from daily movement (walking, stairs).

2. **Day 3 — Moderate cardiovascular training:** 25-30 minutes at an intensity above Zone 2 but below all-out effort. Improves lactate clearance and cardiac efficiency. Can be a tempo run, moderate rowing session, or sustained bike effort.

3. **Day 5 — HIIT:** Short, intense intervals (e.g., 4x4 minutes hard with 4 minutes rest, or 8x30 seconds with 30 seconds rest). Builds VO2 max, stroke volume, and anaerobic capacity.

> "The core elements of it are that it includes at least 150 minutes and ideally more like 200 minutes per week of so-called zone 2 cardio... In addition to that, a foundational or optimal fitness program for most people is going to include anywhere from two to four cardiovascular training sessions that are separate from the zone 2 cardio, as well as two to four resistance or strength training sessions." — Andrew Huberman, LYYyQcAJZfk.md

### The Resistance Training Layer

Three sessions cover the full body with a split that avoids direct overlap:

1. **Day 2 — Legs:** The largest muscle groups, trained fresh after a rest-like Zone 2 day. Compound movements (squats, deadlifts) first, isolation (leg curls, calf raises) second.

2. **Day 4 — Torso:** Chest, shoulders, back, and neck. Push/pull supersets are efficient here. Includes overhead pressing, rowing variations, pull-ups or lat pulldowns, and direct neck work.

3. **Day 6 — Small body parts:** Biceps, triceps, calves, forearms. These muscles get indirect work on Days 2 and 4, so Day 6 provides direct, focused volume. Higher rep ranges (8-15) and shorter rest periods (60-90 seconds) are appropriate.

Each resistance session is 45-75 minutes. All sessions should end with 3-5 minutes of down-regulation breathing (slow exhales or physiological sighs) to shift from sympathetic to parasympathetic state and accelerate recovery.

### Day 7 — Thermal Stress and Recovery

The seventh day combines deliberate cold exposure and deliberate heat exposure (sauna), plus light walking or mobility work. This is not a rest day in the traditional sense — it is an active recovery day using thermal stress for hormetic benefit.

### Customization Rules

This template is designed to be modified, not followed rigidly:

- **Shift the start day:** Day 1 does not need to be Monday. Start the cycle on whatever day works.
- **Swap order within constraints:** Cardio and resistance days can be reordered, but avoid placing two resistance sessions targeting overlapping muscle groups on consecutive days.
- **Scale volume:** Beginners may reduce session duration and volume. Advanced trainees may add sets or a second weekly session per muscle group.
- **Deload every 12-16 weeks:** Take one week of light activity (hiking, walking, mobility) to allow accumulated fatigue to dissipate.

### Caveats

- This template is not designed for athletes specializing in one modality (powerlifters, marathoners, competitive swimmers). Specialists need periodized programs.
- Adequate sleep (7-9 hours) is required to sustain this training volume. Sleep deprivation increases injury risk and blunts adaptation.
- After 10 days without any training, measurable brain health decline begins (reduced cerebral blood flow and oxygenation).
- The schedule assumes reasonable fitness. Someone returning from a long layoff should start with 3-4 days per week and build up over 4-6 weeks.

## Example

A 40-year-old father of two works a desk job Monday through Friday. He maps the template as follows: Sunday = Day 1 (60-minute morning hike with family, Zone 2). Monday = Day 2 (legs at the gym, 6am before work). Tuesday = Day 3 (25-minute jog at lunch, moderate pace). Wednesday = Day 4 (torso at the gym, 6am). Thursday = Day 5 (20-minute HIIT on assault bike, lunchtime). Friday = Day 6 (arms and calves at the gym, 6am). Saturday = Day 7 (sauna at the gym, cold shower at home, family walk). He supplements Zone 2 by walking during phone calls and taking stairs at the office, accumulating an additional 60-80 minutes of Zone 2 per week beyond the Sunday hike.

## Output

A person following this protocol consistently should experience:
- Comprehensive cardiovascular fitness across all energy systems
- Maintained or increased muscle mass across all major muscle groups
- Improved body composition (increased lean mass, reduced fat mass)
- Protected brain health through regular BDNF release and cerebrovascular maintenance
- A sustainable, repeatable weekly structure that prevents the common failure mode of ad hoc, inconsistent training
- A template that can be followed for decades with periodic adjustments
