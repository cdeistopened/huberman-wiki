## When to Use

Apply this framework when a user is comparing protocols, deciding whether a supplement or behavior change is worth adopting, or feeling overwhelmed by the number of Huberman recommendations. It forces a structured evaluation instead of "try everything."

## The Value Equation (Adapted for Health)

Hormozi's original equation from *$100M Offers*:

```
Value = (Dream Outcome x Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
```

Applied to health protocols, each variable maps cleanly:

| Variable | Business Meaning | Health Meaning | How to Score |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|--------------|
| **Dream Outcome** | Revenue, status | The health goal (better sleep, more focus, less anxiety) | How important is this outcome to you? (1-10) |
| **Likelihood of Achievement** | Probability of success | Evidence quality + individual fit | RCT-backed = 8-10; observational = 5-7; anecdotal = 2-4 |
| **Time Delay** | How long until results | Protocol duration before measurable improvement | Days = 1; weeks = 3; months = 6; years = 10 |
| **Effort & Sacrifice** | Compliance cost | Daily time, discomfort, dietary restriction, cost | Minimal = 1; moderate = 4; significant = 7; extreme = 10 |

Higher numerator, lower denominator = higher value protocol.

## Scoring Huberman Protocols (Examples)

### Morning Sunlight (10 min/day)
- Dream Outcome: 8 (circadian health affects sleep, mood, energy)
- Likelihood: 9 (strong RCT evidence, Huberman's most-cited recommendation)
- Time Delay: 2 (noticeable within 3-5 days)
- Effort: 1 (walk outside, free)
- **Score: 72/2 = 36** (extremely high value)

### Cold Exposure (11 min/week)
- Dream Outcome: 7 (dopamine, mood, resilience)
- Likelihood: 8 (strong evidence for dopamine, good for mood)
- Time Delay: 1 (immediate dopamine effect, same day)
- Effort: 6 (uncomfortable, requires setup or gym access)
- **Score: 56/6 = 9.3** (high value, but effort is real)

### Tongkat Ali + Fadogia (daily supplement)
- Dream Outcome: 5 (hormonal optimization, moderate impact)
- Likelihood: 5 (limited human RCT data, mostly animal studies for fadogia)
- Time Delay: 4 (weeks to notice, needs blood work to verify)
- Effort: 3 (pill, cycling protocol, cost, blood work)
- **Score: 25/12 = 2.1** (moderate value, evidence is thinner)

## How to Use in Practice

When a user asks "should I do X?" or "what should I prioritize?", score each option:

1. Ask: What's the health outcome you care about most? (Dream Outcome)
2. Check the evidence tier from the archive (Likelihood)
3. Note how long before they'll feel results (Time Delay)
4. Assess the daily compliance burden (Effort)

**The insight:** Most people overweight Dream Outcome and underweight Effort. A supplement with a big promise but a complex cycling protocol and blood work requirement has lower real value than a free behavior change (sunlight, caffeine delay) with strong evidence.

## Evidence Tiers (Huberman's Implicit Hierarchy)

Huberman doesn't use this exact taxonomy, but his level of confidence maps to evidence quality:

| Tier | Evidence Type | Huberman's Language | Likelihood Score |
|------|--------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| 1 | Multiple RCTs, meta-analyses | "The data are very clear..." | 8-10 |
| 2 | Single strong RCT or multiple observational | "There's good evidence..." | 6-8 |
| 3 | Animal studies + mechanism | "In animal models... the mechanism suggests..." | 4-6 |
| 4 | Anecdotal + plausible mechanism | "Many people report... I personally find..." | 2-4 |
| 5 | Speculation | "It's possible that... we don't know yet..." | 1-2 |
