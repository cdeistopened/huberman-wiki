## When to Use

Use this when someone asks about depression, feels depressed, or wants to understand why their antidepressant is or is not working. The critical insight from Huberman's depression episode is that depression is not one disease with one cause. It involves at least three neurochemical systems (norepinephrine, dopamine, serotonin), an inflammatory pathway, and a circadian rhythm disruption — and different people have different primary pathways driving their symptoms. Matching the tool to the pathway is what produces results. This framework maps each pathway to its specific symptoms and corresponding interventions.

## The Protocol

### The Three Neurochemical Systems

Depression disrupts three monoamine neurotransmitter systems, each producing a distinct symptom cluster:

**1. Norepinephrine Depletion: Psychomotor Deficits**
- Symptoms: lethargy, exhaustion, inability to get out of bed, slowed movement and speech, feeling physically heavy
- This is the "can't move" dimension of depression

> "That's the diabolical nature of depression which is if people are far enough along... oftentimes they can't get the energy to even get up and take a bath or a shower." — Andrew Huberman, HWcphoKlbxY.md

- **Tools for norepinephrine pathway:**
  - Cold exposure (200-300% norepinephrine increase from 4C water for 20 seconds)
  - Exercise (any form, but especially high-intensity)
  - Morning sunlight (triggers cortisol awakening response, which activates norepinephrine cascades)

**2. Dopamine Depletion: Anhedonia**
- Symptoms: inability to experience pleasure, loss of motivation, nothing feels rewarding, apathy, social withdrawal
- This is the "nothing matters" dimension of depression

- **Tools for dopamine pathway:**
  - Do NOT overwhelm the pleasure system with stimulants or easy rewards (this worsens the deficit)
  - Deliberate cold exposure (produces long-lasting dopamine elevation — hours, not minutes)
  - NSDR/Yoga Nidra (30-minute session shown to increase striatal dopamine)
  - Avoid dopamine-depleting behaviors: excessive social media, pornography, substance use

**3. Serotonin Depletion: Grief, Guilt, and Cognitive Symptoms**
- Symptoms: pervasive sadness, guilt, self-blame, negative self-narratives, cognitive distortion, ruminative thinking
- This is the "everything is my fault" dimension of depression

- **Tools for serotonin pathway:**
  - EPA omega-3 fatty acids: >1000mg EPA specifically (the EPA fraction is what matters for mood, not DHA). Ideally 1000-2000mg/day
  - Exercise (increases serotonin synthesis)
  - SSRIs (work for approximately 2/3 of patients; effect delayed ~2 weeks despite immediate serotonin action)

### The Inflammatory Pathway

This is the pathway most people do not know about, and Huberman considers it one of the most important.

Chronic inflammation diverts tryptophan (the precursor to serotonin) away from serotonin production and into a neurotoxic pathway: tryptophan → kynurenine → quinolinic acid. Quinolinic acid is directly neurotoxic. This means that in the presence of chronic inflammation, the brain cannot make enough serotonin even if the raw materials are available.

**Tools for the inflammatory pathway:**
- EPA omega-3s (1000-2000mg/day): reduce inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, TNF-alpha, C-reactive protein)
- Exercise: sequesters kynurenine into muscle tissue, preventing its conversion to the neurotoxic quinolinic acid in the brain
- Ketogenic diet: increases GABA, may help treatment-resistant depression (limited but promising data; Dr. Chris Palmer's research)

### The Circadian Pathway

Depression has a distinct cortisol signature: a 9pm cortisol peak, instead of the healthy pattern (peak 30-45 minutes after waking, tapering through the day).

> "A 9:00 p.m. peak in cortisol is one of the physiological signatures of depressive-like states." — Andrew Huberman, HWcphoKlbxY.md

**Tools for circadian pathway:**
- Morning sunlight within 30-60 minutes of waking
- Delay caffeine 90-120 minutes after waking
- Dim lights in evening
- Avoid late-night high-intensity exercise
- NSDR upon waking (see Burnout Recovery Protocol)

### Supplemental Tools

| Supplement | Dosage | Mechanism | Evidence |
|-----------|--------|-----------|----------|
| EPA Omega-3 | >1000mg EPA/day (ideally 2000mg) | Reduces inflammatory cytokines, supports serotonin | Multiple RCTs |
| Creatine monohydrate | 3-5g/day | Augments SSRI effectiveness via phosphocreatine system in forebrain | RCT in JAMA Psychiatry (2012) |
| Exercise | 150+ min/week | Sequesters kynurenine, increases all three monoamines | Extensive evidence base |
| Cold exposure | 11 min/week across 2-4 sessions | 200-300% norepinephrine increase, sustained dopamine elevation | Multiple studies |

### Sleep Architecture Disruption

Depression characteristically disrupts sleep in specific ways:
- Slow-wave/REM ratio is inverted (too much REM relative to slow-wave)
- Early morning waking (3-4 AM) is common
- 20% of depressed patients have low thyroid hormone (check thyroid function)

### Anti-Self-Confabulation

Depressed patients construct negative narratives that do not match reality. This is not a choice or a personality flaw — it is a symptom of prefrontal cortical dysfunction. The brain generates false explanatory narratives that are uniformly negative. The Pennebaker journaling protocol directly targets this by strengthening prefrontal coherence.

### Key Statistics

- 5% of the population is affected by major depression
- Depression is the #4 cause of disability worldwide
- Genetic component: 50% concordance in identical twins, 25% in siblings, 10% in half-siblings
- 4-5 major stressful life episodes dramatically increase depression risk
- SSRIs work for approximately 2/3 of patients

### Caveats

- Exercise is both protective and therapeutic, BUT severely depressed people often cannot access the motivation to exercise — this is not laziness, it is norepinephrine depletion
- SSRIs are a contested topic — side effects vary enormously between individuals, and effects can change over time
- The creatine study was conducted specifically in women with major depressive disorder — results may not generalize identically
- Ketogenic diet data is limited and should be discussed with a physician
- This framework is educational, not diagnostic. Depression requires professional evaluation and treatment.
- Supplements are tools, not replacements for foundational practices and professional care

## Example

A 32-year-old woman describes her depression as primarily "I can't get off the couch, nothing sounds good, and I wake up at 3am every night." Using this framework, her symptom profile maps to: norepinephrine depletion (can't move), dopamine depletion (anhedonia), and circadian disruption (3am waking). She starts with the lowest-barrier interventions: morning sunlight within 30 minutes of waking (circadian), 1500mg EPA fish oil daily (anti-inflammatory + serotonin support), and a 10-minute NSDR upon waking (dopamine replenishment). After 2 weeks, she has enough energy for a 20-minute walk, which she does in the morning with panoramic vision. The 3am waking resolves first (week 1), followed by gradually increasing motivation (weeks 2-4). She discusses adding creatine supplementation with her psychiatrist.

## Output

After identifying and targeting the specific depressive pathway(s) for 4-8 weeks, a person should experience:
- Improvement in the symptom cluster matching their primary pathway
- For norepinephrine pathway: increased energy, ability to initiate activity
- For dopamine pathway: return of pleasure from everyday activities, renewed motivation
- For serotonin pathway: reduced guilt, less ruminative thinking, more balanced self-narrative
- For inflammatory pathway: reduced general malaise, improved cognitive clarity
- For circadian pathway: normalized sleep timing, reduced early morning waking
- A framework for understanding why specific interventions do or do not work for their particular presentation
