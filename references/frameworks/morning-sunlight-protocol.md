## When to Use

Use this when someone asks about morning energy, circadian rhythm, waking up groggy, seasonal depression, or how to set their internal clock. This is the single most-repeated protocol across all of Huberman Lab — he considers it the foundation of all other sleep and energy optimization.

## The Protocol

The morning sunlight protocol triggers the Cortisol Awakening Response at the correct time by sending light through the eyes to melanopsin ganglion cells, which signal the suprachiasmatic nucleus (the master circadian clock). Getting this right anchors the entire 24-hour cycle: alertness during the day, melatonin release at night, and consistent sleep onset.

### Step 1: Get Outside Within 30-60 Minutes of Waking

Go outdoors. Do not attempt to get this light through a window or windshield — glass filters out too many of the wavelengths that drive the circadian response. Corrective eyeglasses and contact lenses are fine (they focus light onto the retina). Do not wear sunglasses.

> "View bright light, ideally from sunlight, within the first 30 to 60 minutes after waking... You want to trigger that cortisol increase to occur very early in your day, and you don't want that cortisol peak to happen later, which is what will happen if you wait to get outside and see sunlight." — Andrew Huberman, h2aWYjSA1Jc.md

### Step 2: Look Toward the Sun (Not Directly At It)

Face the general direction of the sun. If it is too bright to look near it comfortably, offset your gaze 10-20 degrees. Blink naturally. Never look at any light so bright it causes pain.

### Step 3: Duration Depends on Conditions

The amount of time required scales with cloud cover because photon density varies dramatically:

| Condition | Duration | Approximate Lux |
|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| Clear, bright day | 3-7 minutes (target ~5 min) | ~100,000 lux |
| Cloudy day | ~10 minutes | ~10,000 lux |
| Densely overcast or rainy | 20-30 minutes | ~1,000-5,000 lux |

> "Even on a very overcast day, there's a lot of photon energy coming through... Compare how bright it is at 9:00 a.m. versus midnight the night before." — Andrew Huberman, iT8W6kaD-RA.md

For reference, bright artificial indoor lighting delivers roughly 1,000 lux, and typical room lighting only 100-200 lux. Neither comes close to outdoor light, even on an overcast day.

### Step 4: If You Wake Before Sunrise

Turn on as many bright artificial lights as possible in your home. This is not a full substitute for sunlight, but it provides a partial signal. Then get outside once the sun rises to complete the protocol.

### The Science in Brief

> "This is not some woo biology thing. This is grounded in the core of our physiology. There are literally hundreds, if not thousands, of quality peer-reviewed papers showing that light is the most powerful stimulus for wakefulness throughout the day and it has a powerful, positive impact on your ability to fall and stay asleep at night." — Andrew Huberman, h2aWYjSA1Jc.md

The mechanism: sunlight activates intrinsically photosensitive retinal ganglion cells (ipRGCs) containing melanopsin. These cells are most sensitive to the blue-yellow contrast present in low-angle morning sunlight. They send signals to the suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN), which triggers a pulse of cortisol — the Cortisol Awakening Response. This cortisol pulse sets the timer for melatonin release approximately 12-14 hours later.

### Caveats

- Never look at any light that causes pain or discomfort.
- Artificial light through ring lights, SAD lamps, or bright overhead fixtures is a poor substitute but better than nothing when outdoor light is unavailable.
- Corrective lenses with UV protection are fine — enough relevant wavelengths still penetrate.
- Consistency matters more than perfection. Doing this 5 out of 7 days per week is substantially better than 0.

## Example

A remote worker in Seattle wakes at 6:30am in January. Sunrise is not until 7:45am. She turns on all overhead lights and a desk lamp immediately upon waking. At 7:50am, she steps outside for a 20-minute walk (overcast conditions require longer exposure). By 8:10am, the cortisol pulse has been triggered, setting her circadian clock. She notices improved afternoon energy and earlier sleep onset within the first week.

## Output

After applying this protocol consistently for 3-7 days, a person should experience:
- More alertness in the first 2-3 hours after waking (without relying on caffeine)
- Earlier and more reliable melatonin onset in the evening
- Improved sleep onset timing and reduced "second wind" at night
- A stable anchor point for the entire circadian system
