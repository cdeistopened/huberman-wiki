## Magnesium Threonate (Magnesium L-Threonate)

**Domain:** Nutrition & Supplements
**Source Episodes:** h2aWYjSA1Jc, Ibj1k3IZTNU, xGmGBFpmdhQ, Y3NUEPNZDMA
**Guest (if any):** Solo episodes (Huberman)

### Recommendation
Core component of Huberman's "sleep stack." Magnesium threonate is preferred over other forms because it crosses the blood-brain barrier more effectively. Huberman has taken it nightly for 8+ years. Also discussed in the cortisol episode as potentially helping suppress cortisol indirectly.

### Dosage & Timing
- **Dosage:** 145 mg of magnesium threonate
- **Form:** Magnesium L-threonate (preferred); magnesium bisglycinate is interchangeable for sleep but threonate is "better for a variety of reasons"
- **Timing:** 30-60 minutes before bedtime
- **With food?:** Works best on relatively empty stomach (ideally haven't eaten for ~3 hours); works even after late meals
- **Cycling:** No cycling recommended; Huberman takes it nightly

### Key Quotes
> "145 milligrams of magnesium threonate. 50 milligrams of apigenin. And 100 to 400 milligrams of theanine taken, again, alone or in combination with the other supplements mentioned in the stack many people find allows them to get really drowsy and fall asleep, sleep really deeply, and they feel much more refreshed the next day and they don't have a grogginess to them." -- Huberman, h2aWYjSA1Jc

> "About 5% of people report that magnesium threonate really disrupts their gut. It gives them diarrhea or gastric distress. In which case, don't take it. The proper dosage for you, in other words, would be zero milligrams." -- Huberman, h2aWYjSA1Jc

> "There's evidence that magnesium L-threonate can indirectly activate pathways that might further help suppress cortisol." -- Huberman, Ibj1k3IZTNU

### Caveats & Warnings
- ~5% of people get GI distress (diarrhea) -- discontinue if this happens
- Start with single-ingredient formulations so you can adjust dosages independently
- Consult physician before adding any supplement

### Cross-Links
- Used for: sleep, cortisol reduction, cognitive function
- Part of: Huberman sleep stack (with apigenin + theanine)

## Apigenin

**Domain:** Nutrition & Supplements
**Source Episodes:** h2aWYjSA1Jc, Ibj1k3IZTNU, Y3NUEPNZDMA
**Guest (if any):** Solo episodes (Huberman)

### Recommendation
Derived from chamomile. Part of the sleep stack. Also acts as a cortisol reducer via GABA and chloride channel pathways. Huberman has taken it nightly for 8+ years.

### Dosage & Timing
- **Dosage:** 50 mg
- **Form:** Concentrated capsule (from chamomile)
- **Timing:** 30-60 minutes before bedtime
- **With food?:** Ideally on relatively empty stomach
- **Cycling:** No cycling; nightly use

### Key Quotes
> "Apigenin because it works on both the GABA pathway and some chloride channel pathways has been shown to decrease cortisol." -- Huberman, Ibj1k3IZTNU

> "I take 50 milligrams of apigenin in capsule form every night before I go to sleep. I've been doing that for well over eight years now and will continue to because I find it's very helpful for my sleep." -- Huberman, Ibj1k3IZTNU

### Caveats & Warnings
- Cortisol-lowering effect is less dramatic than ashwagandha
- Can be taken alone or as part of the full sleep stack
- Women of childbearing age should be aware apigenin may have mild estrogenic effects (mentioned in other contexts)

### Cross-Links
- Used for: sleep, cortisol reduction
- Part of: Huberman sleep stack

## L-Theanine

**Domain:** Nutrition & Supplements
**Source Episodes:** h2aWYjSA1Jc, Y3NUEPNZDMA
**Guest (if any):** Solo episodes (Huberman)

### Recommendation
Amino acid found in tea. Third component of the sleep stack. Can be taken alone for calm focus during the day or for sleep at night. Some people get vivid dreams that are disruptive.

### Dosage & Timing
- **Dosage:** 100-400 mg (start at 100 mg)
- **Form:** Capsule or powder
- **Timing:** 30-60 minutes before bedtime (for sleep); can also be used during the day for calm focus
- **With food?:** Doesn't matter
- **Cycling:** No cycling recommended

### Key Quotes
> "Many people who can tolerate magnesium threonate or really thrive on magnesium threonate and like apigenin might find that theanine, even at the lowest dose of a hundred milligrams... gives them such vivid dreams that they actually find it disruptive where they wake up in the middle of the night." -- Huberman, h2aWYjSA1Jc

### Caveats & Warnings
- Some people get vivid, anxiety-producing dreams -- leave it out of the stack if this happens
- Start at 100 mg and work up to 400 mg
- Some people should leave theanine out entirely and use only magnesium threonate + apigenin

### Cross-Links
- Used for: sleep, calm focus, anxiety reduction
- Part of: Huberman sleep stack

## Omega-3 Fatty Acids (EPA/DHA)

**Domain:** Nutrition & Supplements
**Source Episodes:** Q4qWzbP0q7I, HWcphoKlbxY, fh2dBmLN-ZM, NtQ-kdLlQiU, h_1zlead9ZU
**Guest (if any):** Dr. Rhonda Patrick (fh2dBmLN-ZM), Dr. John Kruse (NtQ-kdLlQiU), Alan Aragon (h_1zlead9ZU)

### Recommendation
One of Huberman's foundational supplements. The threshold of 1,000+ mg EPA is critical for mood and anti-inflammatory effects. At that dosage, EPA has been shown to be as effective as SSRIs (fluoxetine/Prozac) for depression. Rhonda Patrick recommends 2+ grams total omega-3 and discusses the omega-3 index as a longevity marker.

### Dosage & Timing
- **Dosage:** Minimum 1,000 mg EPA (above and beyond DHA); Rhonda Patrick recommends 2+ grams total omega-3
- **Form:** Triglyceride form preferred (higher bioavailability); ethyl ester form acceptable but take with food. Keep refrigerated to prevent oxidation.
- **Timing:** With food; time of day doesn't matter
- **With food?:** Yes, especially ethyl ester form
- **Cycling:** No cycling; daily use

### Key Quotes
> "A comparison of 1,000 milligrams a day of EPA... compared to 20 milligrams of fluoxetine, which is Prozac. They found that they were equally effective in reducing depressive symptoms. What was really interesting is that the combination of 1,000 milligrams of EPA and fluoxetine had a synergistic effect in lowering depressive symptoms." -- Huberman, Q4qWzbP0q7I

> "The threshold level seems to be about 1 gram, 1,000 milligrams of EPA... what's really important to look at is whether or not there's more than 1,000 milligrams of EPA because the EPA in particular is what's important here." -- Huberman, HWcphoKlbxY

> "People who had an omega-3 index of 4% or lower had a five-year decreased life expectancy compared to people that had an 8% omega-3 index. For people in the 4% range to get to 8%... they needed to supplement with about 2 grams a day." -- Dr. Rhonda Patrick, fh2dBmLN-ZM

> "I think Mother Nature is probably smarter than any Harvard professor... I would say we can still count based on about 1,000 milligrams of EPA, but don't worry that you're getting about 300-400 milligrams of DHA; probably that's better for you." -- Dr. John Kruse, NtQ-kdLlQiU

### Caveats & Warnings
- Must reach 1,000 mg EPA threshold for mood/anti-inflammatory benefits
- Natural ratio is ~2:1 EPA to DHA -- don't seek pure EPA isolates
- Check IFOS (International Fish Oil Standards) for third-party testing: TOTOX score ideally under 6, at least under 10
- Keep fish oil refrigerated to prevent oxidation
- Omega-3 index test (red blood cells) takes 120 days to reflect changes
- EPA lowers inflammation by producing resolvins and specialized pro-resolving molecules (SPMs)

### Cross-Links
- Used for: mood/depression, inflammation reduction, cardiovascular health, brain health, longevity
- Synergistic with: SSRIs (can augment or reduce required dose)

## Creatine Monohydrate

**Domain:** Nutrition & Supplements
**Source Episodes:** LYYyQcAJZfk, hCyvqRq5YmM, q-wRvsiGYIs, h_1zlead9ZU, Ibj1k3IZTNU
**Guest (if any):** Dr. Chris Palmer (hCyvqRq5YmM), Alan Aragon (h_1zlead9ZU)

### Recommendation
Foundational supplement for both physical and cognitive performance. Creatine monohydrate is the only form worth taking. Dosage should be scaled to body weight -- the common "5g for everyone" advice is not well-informed. No need to load. Creatine also shows promise for depression, bipolar disorder, and neurodegenerative conditions (per Dr. Palmer).

### Dosage & Timing
- **Dosage:** Body-weight adjusted: 130-180 lbs = 3-5g/day; 185-250 lbs = 10-15g/day. Huberman personally takes 10-15g/day at ~220 lbs.
- **Form:** Creatine monohydrate (least expensive, most effective; other forms are NOT superior)
- **Timing:** Doesn't matter -- post-workout, pre-workout, or any time of day
- **With food?:** Some people need to take with food to avoid GI distress
- **Cycling:** No cycling needed; no need to "load"

### Key Quotes
> "If you weigh 185 pounds or so... 10 to 15g of creatine per day is probably more appropriate for you than is 5 grams, meaning it's going to be more effective for enhancing physical performance. And perhaps... even cognitive performance as well." -- Huberman, LYYyQcAJZfk

> "I see no evidence whatsoever that the other forms of creatine are superior to creatine monohydrate." -- Huberman, LYYyQcAJZfk

> "We do have randomized controlled trial data with creatine showing that it can improve symptoms of major depression, it can augment antidepressants, it can improve symptoms of bipolar disorder, and it can improve symptoms of neurodegenerative disorders like cognition in Alzheimer's disease." -- Dr. Chris Palmer, hCyvqRq5YmM

> "Our bodies can produce creatine on their own... or we can consume creatine. Creatine is found only in animal-sourced foods; it is not found in plant-sourced foods." -- Dr. Chris Palmer, hCyvqRq5YmM

### Caveats & Warnings
- Some people get GI distress -- start low and increase gradually; take with food
- Vegans/vegetarians tend to have lower creatine levels and may benefit more from supplementation
- No evidence creatine causes hair loss (explicitly addressed by Huberman)
- Huberman considers it safe enough even for those under 18 (with parental guidance)
- Phosphocreatine system fuels both muscles and brain neurons

### Cross-Links
- Used for: physical performance, cognitive function, depression, mitochondrial health, brain energy
- Interaction with: exercise (resistance training), brain fuel systems

## Caffeine

**Domain:** Nutrition & Supplements
**Source Episodes:** h2aWYjSA1Jc, Ibj1k3IZTNU, LYYyQcAJZfk, K-TW2Chpz4k
**Guest (if any):** Solo episodes (Huberman)

### Recommendation
Huberman's preferred source is yerba mate. The famous "delay caffeine 90-120 minutes" recommendation is nuanced -- Huberman himself does NOT always follow it, especially on training days. Key insight: for chronic caffeine users, caffeine doesn't significantly spike cortisol but extends its duration, which is useful for sustained morning energy. Cutoff 8-10 hours before bed.

### Dosage & Timing
- **Dosage:** Individual; Huberman drinks ~800+ mg/day. Most people use 100-400 mg.
- **Form:** Yerba mate (Huberman's preference), coffee, tea
- **Timing:** Ideal: delay 90-120 minutes after waking to extend morning cortisol peak. Exception: if training early, caffeine before workout is fine. Cutoff: 8-10 hours before bedtime, at minimum by early afternoon (~2 PM).
- **With food?:** Not critical
- **Cycling:** Not discussed as necessary

### Key Quotes
> "If you're somebody who regularly drinks caffeine... it's not going to boost your cortisol levels that much... However, it will prolong the duration of cortisol's effectiveness in your bloodstream." -- Huberman, Ibj1k3IZTNU

> "I've tried to be clear about this in previous episodes, but I think a number of people have come to think that I always delay my caffeine intake 90 to 120 minutes after waking, and that's simply not the case." -- Huberman, LYYyQcAJZfk

### Caveats & Warnings
- The 90-120 minute delay is a useful guideline but not a rigid rule
- Chronic users get less cortisol spike but prolonged cortisol effect
- Non-habitual users will get a significant cortisol spike
- Afternoon caffeine (after ~2 PM) disrupts sleep architecture
- Can cause an afternoon crash if morning cortisol peak drops too fast -- delaying caffeine can help with this

### Cross-Links
- Used for: wakefulness, focus, extending morning cortisol peak, pre-workout performance
- Interactions: adenosine system, cortisol rhythm

## Ashwagandha (KSM-66 / Withania somnifera)

**Domain:** Nutrition & Supplements
**Source Episodes:** Ibj1k3IZTNU
**Guest (if any):** Solo episode (Huberman)

### Recommendation
Effective cortisol reducer (11-29% reduction in studies). Must be taken in the evening/night only -- taking 300+ mg in the morning would lower the cortisol peak you want. Huberman takes 300 mg in the evening. Note: small amounts in products like AG1 are fine in the morning.

### Dosage & Timing
- **Dosage:** 300 mg (start here); studies explored up to 900 mg. Always seek minimal effective dose.
- **Form:** Not specified (KSM-66 is a common standardized extract)
- **Timing:** Evening only -- anytime after dinner. Effects last many hours.
- **With food?:** With or after dinner
- **Cycling:** Not explicitly discussed in these transcripts

### Key Quotes
> "If you look at the literature on ashwagandha, you will see that indeed it can be effective in reducing cortisol levels by anywhere from about 11% to 29%." -- Huberman, Ibj1k3IZTNU

> "Restrict your intake of ashwagandha to the late afternoon and evening and nighttime hours to keep your cortisol levels low and avoid taking dosages of ashwagandha certainly as high as 300 milligrams anytime early in the day." -- Huberman, Ibj1k3IZTNU

> "Anything less than say 50 or 100 milligrams early in the day is not going to have a significant impact on cortisol." -- Huberman, Ibj1k3IZTNU

### Caveats & Warnings
- Do NOT take 300+ mg in the morning -- it will blunt your beneficial morning cortisol spike
- Small amounts (< 50-100 mg) in blended products like AG1 are fine in the morning
- Always do behavioral interventions first (dim lights, reduce stress, etc.) before adding ashwagandha
- Thyroid interactions not explicitly discussed in these episodes but widely noted elsewhere
- Chronic use concerns exist (Huberman acknowledges supplementation should complement, not replace, behavioral tools)

### Cross-Links
- Used for: cortisol reduction, evening relaxation, stress management
- Timing critical: evening only at therapeutic doses

## L-Tyrosine

**Domain:** Nutrition & Supplements
**Source Episodes:** CQlTmOFM4Qs, Q4qWzbP0q7I, K-TW2Chpz4k
**Guest (if any):** Solo episodes (Huberman)

### Recommendation
Amino acid precursor to dopamine. Can improve working memory and focus, especially in multitasking environments. Huberman personally feels effects at 500 mg, though study doses used up to 150 mg/kg (extremely high). Start very low. Prone to a crash 3-4 hours later.

### Dosage & Timing
- **Dosage:** 250-500 mg to start (minimal effective dose). Study doses were 150 mg/kg (up to 15 grams) -- Huberman considers this far too high to recommend.
- **Form:** L-crystalline tyrosine (capsule or powder)
- **Timing:** Before focus sessions; not late in the day
- **With food?:** Can be taken in applesauce or similar (as in the study)
- **Cycling:** Do not take chronically -- can disrupt dopamine pathways with prolonged use

### Key Quotes
> "I've taken 1,000 or 1,500 milligrams of L-tyrosine and I've definitely experienced an increase in alertness... I can feel a meaningful increase in alertness and focus from 500 milligrams of L-tyrosine." -- Huberman, CQlTmOFM4Qs

> "Some people, not all, but some people experience a bit of a crash after L-tyrosine supplementation... three or four hours later experience kind of a drop." -- Huberman, CQlTmOFM4Qs

> "Taken chronically, however, it can disrupt those dopamine pathways." -- Huberman, Q4qWzbP0q7I

### Caveats & Warnings
- Do NOT take chronically -- disrupts dopamine pathways
- 3-4 hour crash is common
- Start at 250-500 mg; the study dose of 150 mg/kg is far too high for general use
- People on MAO inhibitors should NOT take L-tyrosine
- Behavioral tools for dopamine (cold exposure, etc.) are preferred for long-term use because they create slower, more sustained dopamine increases

### Cross-Links
- Used for: working memory, focus, dopamine, alertness
- Caution: crash effect, do not use chronically

## Rhodiola Rosea

Cortisol modulator (not a suppressor or booster). Reduces perceived exertion during high-intensity training and improves recovery. Huberman takes it before 2-3 high-intensity workouts per week. Not a foundational supplement -- it's an "edge" tool.

- **Form:** Capsule

- **With food?:** Not critical
- **Cycling:** Only before high-intensity sessions (2-3x/week); not daily

### Key Quotes
> "Rhodiola rosea is a supplement that's gaining increasing attention because it is what's called a cortisol modulator. It does not necessarily suppress cortisol. It does not increase cortisol. It's a cortisol modulator." -- Huberman, LYYyQcAJZfk

> "My experience has been that when I take Rhodiola rosea I definitely notice that I can exert myself harder without feeling like I'm bringing myself to the brink of fatigue either during the exertion or afterwards." -- Huberman, LYYyQcAJZfk

### Caveats & Warnings
- Not a foundational supplement -- get nutrition, sleep, and sunlight right first

- Don't take it before low-intensity work (not necessary)

### Cross-Links
- Used for: exercise performance, perceived exertion reduction, recovery

## Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia Jack)

**Domain:** Nutrition & Supplements
**Source Episodes:** v4HRWgwjP_k, U3cSnzdyyXU, 6ZrlsVx85ek
**Guest (if any):** Solo (v4HRWgwjP_k)

### Recommendation
Shown to increase free testosterone by liberating bound testosterone. Also has subtle anti-estrogenic and aphrodisiac effects. Huberman personally used it from mid-30s to mid-40s with blood-work-confirmed results.

### Dosage & Timing
- **Dosage:** 400-800 mg/day (range in literature)
- **Form:** Capsule
- **Timing:** Earlier in the day; can cause insomnia if taken too late
- **With food?:** Not specified
- **Cycling:** Not explicitly stated in transcripts

### Key Quotes
> "There is some decent scientific literature to support the fact that it liberates some of the bound testosterone and allows more free testosterone to be available." -- Huberman, v4HRWgwjP_k

> "Some of the reported side effects are things like excessive alertness and insomnia if it's taken too late in the day." -- Huberman, v4HRWgwjP_k

> "I got great results all through my mid-30s until mid-40s, and still with Tongkat Ali freeing some testosterone up -- my blood charts told me that worked for me, though it may not work for everybody." -- Huberman, U3cSnzdyyXU

### Caveats & Warnings
- Can cause insomnia if taken late in the day
- Any hormone-modulating supplement requires blood work monitoring
- Any tissue that undergoes rapid cell reproduction (prostate, testes, ovaries) is vulnerable to cancers that thrive on androgens -- be cautious with hormone optimization
- Effects are "far more subtle" than injecting testosterone
- Must get blood work done when taking any supplement that modulates sex hormones

### Cross-Links
- Used for: free testosterone, libido, mild anti-estrogen
- Requires: blood work monitoring

## Fadogia Agrestis

**Domain:** Nutrition & Supplements
**Source Episodes:** v4HRWgwjP_k, U3cSnzdyyXU
**Guest (if any):** Solo (v4HRWgwjP_k)

### Recommendation
Can increase luteinizing hormone (LH), which in turn increases testosterone and estrogen production. Works at a different level than Tongkat Ali -- Fadogia acts on the brain-pituitary axis rather than just freeing bound testosterone. Side effect profile is poorly documented.

### Dosage & Timing
- **Dosage:** Not specifically stated in available transcripts
- **Form:** Capsule
- **Timing:** Not specified
- **With food?:** Not specified
- **Cycling:** Not specified

### Key Quotes
> "Fadogia agrestis... at least according to the literature that I was able to find, can increase levels of luteinizing hormone and thereby levels of testosterone or levels of estrogen. The side effect profile of Fadogia agrestis hasn't really been documented, so it's a little unclear." -- Huberman, v4HRWgwjP_k

> "Fadogia Agrestis, things like that. Subtle effects but meaningful." -- Huberman, U3cSnzdyyXU

### Caveats & Warnings
- Side effect profile NOT well documented
- Same cancer/hormone safety warnings as Tongkat Ali apply
- Blood work is essential when modulating LH and sex steroid hormones
- Negative feedback risk: if testosterone goes too high, LH can be shut down via feedback loops
- Huberman strongly emphasizes monitoring cycles and hormone panels

### Cross-Links
- Used for: testosterone support (via LH), fertility
- Often paired with: Tongkat Ali (complementary mechanisms)

## Protein Intake Protocol

**Domain:** Nutrition & Supplements
**Source Episodes:** q-wRvsiGYIs, h_1zlead9ZU, LYYyQcAJZfk
**Guest (if any):** Alan Aragon (h_1zlead9ZU)

### Recommendation
Approximately 1 gram of quality protein per pound of body weight (or lean body weight) per day. Leucine content is the key differentiator between protein sources. Whey protein is superior for muscle protein synthesis; collagen/bone broth is superior for skin health (at 15g/day). The "30g per meal" limit is a myth -- assimilation depends on protein type, meal composition, and individual variation.

### Dosage & Timing
- **Dosage:** ~1 gram per pound of body weight per day (Huberman gets 175-210g at ~220 lbs)
- **Form:** 60-70% from whole foods (meat, chicken, eggs, fish); 30-50% from protein powder/bars if needed
- **Timing:** Distributed across meals; timing around workouts is less critical than total daily intake
- **With food?:** N/A (is food)
- **Cycling:** N/A

### Key Quotes
> "I believe that most people who are seeking muscle protein synthesis, recovery from exercise, and general health would do well to ingest approximately one gram of quality protein per pound of lean body weight or desired body weight." -- Huberman, q-wRvsiGYIs

> "Whey protein contains relatively high amounts of the amino acid leucine. Therefore, it is going to be the superior form of protein if your goal is to grow muscle, get stronger, or repair muscle damage." -- Huberman, q-wRvsiGYIs

> "People who regularly ingest collagen protein can observe some improvements in skin elasticity and appearance... at levels of 15 grams per day over a period of about two weeks or more." -- Huberman, q-wRvsiGYIs

### Caveats & Warnings
- Whey protein may increase acne in some people (via leucine/mTOR pathway), especially in women at certain menstrual phases
- If acne is an issue, try removing whey for 2 weeks to test
- The "30g per meal assimilation limit" is not accurate -- depends on protein type, speed of digestion, and meal context (per Alan Aragon)
- Collagen protein is NOT a good substitute for whey when the goal is muscle protein synthesis

### Cross-Links
- Used for: muscle growth, recovery, skin health (collagen), general health
- Leucine is the key amino acid for muscle protein synthesis

## Fermented Foods & Gut Microbiome

**Domain:** Nutrition & Supplements
**Source Episodes:** n_MVhE63ZQQ, Q4qWzbP0q7I
**Guest (if any):** Dr. Justin Sonnenburg (n_MVhE63ZQQ)

### Recommendation
Based on the Sonnenburg lab study at Stanford: 4-6+ servings per day of fermented foods increases microbiome diversity and reduces inflammatory markers (IL-6, IL-12, and ~2 dozen others). This was more effective than a high-fiber diet for reducing inflammation in the study. Low-sugar fermented foods are key.

### Dosage & Timing
- **Dosage:** 4-6+ servings per day (study average was 6+ at peak)
- **Form:** Sauerkraut, kimchi, unsweetened yogurt, kombucha, kefir, natto
- **Timing:** ~2 servings per meal, spread throughout the day
- **With food?:** Is food
- **Cycling:** Daily, sustained over weeks for best results

### Key Quotes
> "People during the height of the intervention phase were up over six servings on average per day of fermented foods -- kind of two servings at each meal... We saw this increase in microbiota diversity over the course of the six weeks." -- Dr. Justin Sonnenburg, n_MVhE63ZQQ

> "We saw a couple dozen immune markers -- inflammatory markers -- decrease over the course of the study... things like interleukin-6 and interleukin-12." -- Dr. Justin Sonnenburg, n_MVhE63ZQQ

> "A huge pitfall in this area is you can have a yogurt loaded with bacteria -- the base of what's healthy -- and then a ton of artificial flavoring and sugar loaded on top of that." -- Dr. Justin Sonnenburg, n_MVhE63ZQQ

### Caveats & Warnings
- Avoid sweetened/sugared fermented foods -- manufacturers add sugar to mask sour taste
- Excessive probiotics (capsule form) can cause brain fog -- fermented foods are a safer delivery mechanism
- High fiber alone did NOT produce the same inflammatory reduction as fermented foods in this study
- Making sauerkraut at home (cabbage, water, salt) is cost-effective -- Tim Ferriss's *4-Hour Chef* has a recipe
- Western gut microbiomes have lost diversity over generations of low-fiber diets -- some species may be permanently lost

### Cross-Links
- Used for: gut microbiome diversity, inflammation reduction, immune function
- Superior to: probiotic capsules (for general population), high-fiber diet alone (for inflammation)

## Time-Restricted Eating / Intermittent Fasting

**Domain:** Nutrition & Supplements
**Source Episodes:** hCyvqRq5YmM, f4cdu-QiKHo, wFucddupQlk, Ibj1k3IZTNU
**Guest (if any):** Dr. Chris Palmer (hCyvqRq5YmM)

### Recommendation
Huberman typically eats his first meal around 10:30-11:00 AM. The literature is mixed on time-restricted eating because studies often don't control what people eat. Fasting/ketogenic cycles can improve mitochondrial health through autophagy and mitophagy. The main benefit of morning fasting is sustained cortisol and epinephrine -- low blood glucose increases these alertness hormones.

### Dosage & Timing
- **Window:** Huberman's personal pattern: first meal ~10:30-11 AM; sometimes eats late at night
- **Duration:** Not specified as a rigid protocol
- **Protocol:** Not a strict intermittent fasting advocate -- more "eat when it makes sense for your schedule and goals"

### Key Quotes
> "Can intermittent -- and I'm going to lump ketogenic with other fasting-mimicking diets and fasting itself -- can intermittent fasting or cycles of fasting have health-improving qualities? Absolutely." -- Dr. Chris Palmer, hCyvqRq5YmM

> "When our blood glucose is low, cortisol and epinephrine are going to go up. Anytime we haven't eaten for four to six hours, levels of epinephrine and cortisol are going to go up pretty substantially." -- Huberman, wFucddupQlk

> "When you look at different diets... provided people stick to their particular diet, it doesn't really matter which diet you follow; you can still get a caloric deficit and you get weight loss." -- Huberman, f4cdu-QiKHo

### Caveats & Warnings

- Adherence matters more than the specific diet plan
- Not eating for 2-3 hours before bed is ideal for sleep -- but Huberman acknowledges he sometimes breaks this rule
- Fasting boosts alertness (cortisol + epinephrine) which is why morning fasting works for focus
- Starchy carbohydrates in the evening can help lower cortisol before sleep

### Cross-Links

- Mechanism: autophagy, mitophagy, cortisol/epinephrine regulation
